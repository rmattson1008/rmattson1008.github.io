STEM Outreach

Did you know that most students' path towards STEM will diverge around the 6th grade? And a major barrier to STEM is a students experience with middle school math. Many students face a dry, abstract curriculum that discourages any budding mathematical interest, so I'm always looking for ways to make the math fun as I tutor or help with STEM summer camps. I've led workshops in hands-on activities like robotics and programming raspberry pis - activities that makes the fundamentals seem worth learning. My ultimate goal is to coach the local robotics team once we're all tired of little league.

I've taught web development to highschoolers with Girls Who Code, and I loved it. Being the only women in the room is pretty lame. Getting more women/enbys in STEM can't fix everything, but no-one should miss out on the joys of STEM due to preventable obstacles or hostile environments. Girls Who Code builds confidence in basic programming skills (I think summer 2023 they are teaching game development, if anyone's interested). I learned a lot about the fantastic women who have impacted computer science. Feeling represented helps me to be a more confident, helpful teammate, and I hope to uplift others who are seeking their own role models.
